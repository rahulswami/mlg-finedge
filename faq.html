Personal Loan Questions

What is the minimum salary required for a personal loan in Jaipur?

For salaried professionals in Jaipur, the minimum net monthly salary required ranges from ₹20,000 to ₹25,000, depending on the bank and company category (CAT A/B/C). Government employees and employees of top-tier private firms enjoy relaxed parameters.

Can I get a personal loan if I have a CIBIL score of 650?

While a CIBIL score of 750+ is ideal for personal loans, some NBFC partners do approve loans at a slightly lower score (650-700), but with higher interest rates. We can review your profile to suggest suitable options.

Home Loan Questions

What is FOIR and how does it affect my Home Loan?

FOIR stands for Fixed Income to Obligations Ratio. It measures how much of your monthly income goes toward paying existing debts (EMIs, rent). Banks usually cap FOIR at 50%, meaning they won't approve a loan where total EMIs exceed half of your net salary. For select profiles (like government teachers), we can secure approvals at up to 55% or 60% FOIR.

What documents are required for a Home Loan in Jaipur?

You will need: Identity/address proof (PAN, Aadhaar), 3 months salary slips, 2 years Form 16, 6 months bank statements, and property documents (registry, registry chain, and approved map details).

Business Loan Questions

What is the minimum annual business turnover required?

For unsecured business loans, most lenders require a minimum annual turnover of ₹15 to ₹20 Lakhs, backed by audit reports or GST filings showing active transactions for the past 12 months.

Loan Against Property (LAP) Questions

What type of property is eligible for LAP?

Residential houses, commercial shops, vacant plots, and industrial land with clear ownership titles and approved building blueprints are eligible. Properties with disputes or missing chain documents are generally rejected.

Gold Loan Questions

How is my gold valued for a loan?

Lenders evaluate your gold ornaments for purity (karat) and net weight (excluding stones). Under RBI rules, you can get up to 75% of the appraised market value as a loan (Loan-to-Value or LTV ratio).
